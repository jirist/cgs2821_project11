Educational Qualities of Traditional Toys

Toys do not have to be powered by batteries or electricity to be a lot of fun. Whenever looking for toys and activities for young children, it is important to focus on items that will enhance their growth and development. Toys are not just playthings, but basic learning tools as well. The best toys that foster kid's learning & growth are often the simplest, made in the old fashioned way, made to last.

Back to basics toys every child should play with

Wooden toys

Wooden toys have a place in every playroom, because they’re not only fun and engaging, but great for helping kids develop eye-hand coordination and fine motor skills, too. Packed with tons of fun and designed to stimulate activity and creativity, wooden toys teach children skills for the rest of their lives.

Soft toys

An engaging and entertaining, soft toys stimulate the imagination of kids young and old and make bedtime stories even more fun. Allowing children to engage in meaningful play, pretend play is important for kids of all ages. There is nothing more fun than watching your child make up stories, children can be anyone and do anything in the imaginative play world.

Puzzles & games

Problem solving helps children exercise their brains and enhances their early learning experience. Figuring things out create opportunities for children to practice and increase their social, academic and thinking skills through the process of play.

Traditional playthings

Let's play... it doesn't have to whistle or light up to be fun! Reinforcing basic motor skills, color recognition and counting, toys from a 'simpler time' nurture development in a fun way!

About the Author

Jiri Stanc is a retail store operator, specializing in the specialty toy market and motivated by the conviction that traditional play is an essential part of a child's development. To help kids maintain a healthy balance in their daily lives, technology use should have reasonable limits, viewing digital play as a part of that balance, not a dominating role. The idea is definitely not to stop electronic play but to attain a balance between the two.
